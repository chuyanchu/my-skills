# Deck Blueprints

Pick one blueprint based on the task.

## Single Paper Report

Recommended 8-10 slides:

1. Title: paper title, authors, venue/year if known, presenter/date.
2. One-page takeaway: what the paper is about and why it matters.
3. Problem: task setting, input/output, success criterion.
4. Prior gap: why existing methods are insufficient.
5. Method overview: core idea and agent loop.
6. Key mechanism: planner, memory, tool use, verifier, multi-agent design, or training detail.
7. Evidence: benchmark, baselines, main results, ablations.
8. Limitations: hidden assumptions, failure modes, reproducibility risks.
9. What we can do: follow-up experiments or system integration.
10. Discussion questions: 2-4 questions for the reading group.

## Multi-Paper Comparison

Recommended 10-13 slides:

1. Title: topic and paper group.
2. Why this cluster matters.
3. Paper map: each paper's one-line contribution.
4. Shared problem and different assumptions.
5. Method comparison table.
6. Agent-loop comparison: planning, memory, tools, verifier, collaboration.
7. Evaluation comparison: benchmarks, baselines, ablations, cost.
8. Complementarity: what ideas can be combined.
9. Conflicts and weak evidence.
10. Research gaps.
11. Next reading/reproduction plan.
12. Bottom-line synthesis.
13. Optional discussion or validation experiments: add when the deck is for a reading group or research planning.

## Research Idea Deck

Recommended 6-8 slides:

1. Title: proposed research direction.
2. Motivation from papers.
3. Hypothesis.
4. Proposed method or system.
5. Experiment 1: minimal validation.
6. Experiment 2: ablation or robustness.
7. Expected evidence and risks.
8. Next steps.

## Reproduction Plan

Recommended 7-9 slides:

1. Title: reproduction target.
2. What claim we want to reproduce.
3. Minimal implementation scope.
4. Agent architecture and modules.
5. Data/benchmark/evaluator.
6. Baselines and ablations.
7. Logging: prompts, tool calls, cost, failures.
8. Risks and missing details.
9. Timeline or first experiment.

## Appendix Notes

Use for dense group discussion:

- Keep title and section heading hierarchy.
- Allow 3-4 section blocks per slide.
- Use appendix page markers if more than 5 note slides.
- Move long tables, prompt snippets, and detailed failure taxonomies here.
